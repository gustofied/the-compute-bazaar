Compute is not scarce in one clean place. It is scattered through provider APIs, regional machines, private terms, spot-like availability, and vague promises of performance. The first Compute Bazaar is therefore not an exchange. It is market memory: a repeatable way to capture current offers, preserve their evidence, normalize them into comparable GPU-hour objects, and make them queryable.

IThe Substrate IIThe Markets IIITinkering IVFrictions and Futures

I The Substrate

The substrate is boring on purpose. Bronze keeps raw provider evidence. Silver turns each provider into one shared offer schema. Gold builds product-facing facts: listings, provider floors, index values, regions, and the constituents supporting those values.

II The Markets

The first market view is deliberately simple: show current offers in comparable GPU-hour units, then summarize them by GPU product and provider. The point is not a final benchmark methodology. The point is a reliable path from raw observation to an auditable market object.

IV Frictions and Futures

The public essay should start with static JSON snapshots because static pages are robust, cheap, and safe. Later, a small backend can consume AutoMQ and expose a narrow live feed without putting broker credentials in the browser. Benchmarks, RFQs, and agent execution sit on top of this offer memory; they are not the first layer.

Local dashboard first, AdamSioud exemplar page later. The browser reads public-safe gold snapshots; private provider keys and AutoMQ credentials stay behind the worker boundary.
